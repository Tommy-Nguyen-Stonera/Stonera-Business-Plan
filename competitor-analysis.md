## Competitive Analysis Summary

### What the Best Competitors Do Well (Stonera Should Copy)

1. **Made in Stone's quote form** - The most comprehensive in the industry. Captures dwelling type, application type, stone thickness, waterfall preference, cooktop/sink cutout types, edge profiles, and file uploads. This eliminates 2-3 rounds of back-and-forth emails and gives the fabricator everything needed to provide an accurate quote. Stonera should build a form at least this detailed.

2. **Stone-Tech's content strategy** - Trend articles, comparison guides, care/maintenance guides, and a "Professional Zone" for trade customers. This positions them as experts and drives organic traffic. Their gallery organised by suburb names is clever local SEO.

3. **Royal Stone's suburb service pages** - Individual pages for Tamarama, Vaucluse, Double Bay etc. Each one targets "[stone benchtop] + [suburb]" searches. Easy to replicate and captures local intent traffic.

4. **Marble Benchtops Hub's wishlist quote system** - Customers browse slabs, add them to a wishlist, and then request a quote based on their selections. This is more engaging than a basic contact form and gives the business insight into what materials customers are interested in.

5. **Stone Factory's instant estimator** - An automated quoting tool that gives customers an "accurate estimate within seconds." Even if the final price differs, this gives an immediate anchor and keeps leads on-site instead of bouncing to competitors.

6. **SCY Top Stone's review volume** - 122 five-star reviews and award recognition. This social proof is powerful. Stonera should actively chase reviews after every job.

7. **AUSKstone & Made in Stone's offcuts programs** - Selling stone offcuts at discount prices captures budget-conscious customers who might not afford a full slab. Smart way to reduce waste and generate extra revenue.

8. **Marble Benchtops Hub's "Slabs in Transit" program** - Letting customers see and pre-order slabs before they arrive at the factory. Creates anticipation and locks in sales early.

### Opportunities - Things NO Competitor Does Well That Stonera Could Own

1. **Price transparency.** Almost nobody shows prices. DIY Australia is the only one with online pricing, and they're not a traditional fabricator. Stonera could publish starting-from price ranges for each stone type (e.g., "Caesarstone benchtops from $X/sqm installed") without committing to exact quotes. This would be a massive differentiator and would capture every "how much does a stone benchtop cost in Sydney" search.

2. **Speed guarantee with real timelines.** Benchmark Stonemasons says "5-10 working days" and AK Designer Stone says "5-7 days after measure." Nobody offers a hard guarantee. Stonera could own "measured Monday, installed Friday" or similar with a penalty if they miss the date.

3. **Builder/trade portal.** Stone-Tech has a "Professional Zone" but it appears basic. Nobody has a proper trade login where builders can submit job details, track order status, view pricing tiers, or re-order. Since builders are 40% of Stonera's customer base, a dedicated trade experience could lock in repeat business.

4. **Video content and process transparency.** AUSKstone has some YouTube content but nobody does this well. Factory tour videos, time-lapse of fabrication, "day in the life" content showing the CNC cutting, templating, and installation process. This builds trust and differentiates from competitors who all look the same.

5. **Real-time job tracking.** No competitor offers customers a way to see where their benchtop is in the process (templated > cut > polished > ready for install > installed). This would be especially valuable for builders managing multiple projects.

6. **Cabinet maker partnership program.** Cabinet makers are 30% of Stonera's target. Nobody has a formal referral or partnership program specifically for cabinet makers - just generic "trade" mentions. A co-branded program with pricing incentives, priority scheduling, and perhaps even a shared project management tool could lock this up.

7. **Content in languages other than English.** Sydney has large Chinese, Korean, Arabic, and Vietnamese-speaking communities doing renovations. Nobody has multilingual content. Even basic service pages in 2-3 languages would capture searches competitors cannot.

8. **Same-day or next-day quoting guarantee.** Most competitors just say "request a quote" with no response time commitment. Guaranteeing a quote within 4 business hours or 24 hours would stand out.

9. **Sustainable/waste messaging.** While offcuts are sold by some competitors, nobody positions themselves as environmentally responsible (recycling stone waste, sustainable sourcing, low-energy fabrication). Growing buyer segment cares about this.

10. **Post-installation support content.** Nobody does a good job of staying in touch after the sale. A simple care guide email sequence, 12-month check-in, or maintenance service offering would generate referrals and repeat business.

### Quote Form Best Practices (Based on Competitor Analysis)

The ideal quote form for Stonera should include:

**Essential fields (steal from Made in Stone's form):**
- Name, email, phone, address
- Application type (kitchen, bathroom, laundry, BBQ/outdoor, fireplace, commercial, other)
- Dwelling type (house, unit/apartment, townhouse, granny flat)
- Supply preference (supply & install vs supply only)
- Stone thickness preference (12mm, 20mm, 40mm)

**Technical details that save time:**
- Waterfall edge (yes/no)
- Cooktop cutout type (drop-in, flush mount, freestanding, none)
- Sink cutout type (drop-in, undermount, flush mount, butler sink, none)
- Edge profile preference (visual selector like Made in Stone)
- Approximate benchtop dimensions or total sqm if known

**Smart additions:**
- File upload for plans, photos, or drawings (like Made in Stone and AUSKstone)
- "How did you hear about us?" dropdown for attribution tracking (like Royal Stone)
- Budget range selector (unlike any competitor - this would help Stonera prioritise leads and suggest appropriate materials)
- Preferred timeline/urgency (ASAP, within 2 weeks, within a month, just researching)
- "Are you a builder/trade?" checkbox (triggers trade pricing pathway)

**What NOT to do:**
- Don't have only a basic name/email/message form (Royal Stone's weakness)
- Don't require phone calls for all quotes (Universal Stone's weakness)
- Don't hide behind "contact us for pricing" with zero indication of cost range (everyone's weakness)

**Best UX patterns seen:**
- Marble Benchtops Hub's wishlist/cart approach for slab selection
- Stone Factory's instant estimator for immediate price indication
- Made in Stone's visual edge profile selector
- Multiple quote forms placed throughout the site, not just on one contact page

## Key Takeaways for Stonera

1. **Stonera's biggest competitors in the affordable space** are SCY Top Stone (reviews king), Royal Stone (claims "cheapest"), AUSKstone (factory direct), and Benchmark Stonemasons (value-focused builders). These four fight on price.

2. **The content gap is wide open.** Most competitors have weak or non-existent blogs. Stone-Tech is the only one doing content well. Stonera can overtake everyone except Stone-Tech with 10-15 well-written articles covering trends, comparisons, care guides, and pricing guides.

3. **Nobody owns the builder/trade relationship properly.** Despite builders being a huge referral source, no competitor has a dedicated trade portal, partnership program, or even a proper trade section on their website.

4. **Price transparency is the single biggest untapped opportunity.** In an industry where every single website says "contact us for a quote," being the one fabricator that shows even ballpark pricing would capture an enormous volume of search traffic and build immediate trust.

5. **Review accumulation should be a top priority.** SCY Top Stone proves that 100+ five-star reviews at massive competitive advantage. Stonera should have a systematic process to request a review after every completed job.

6. **The quote form is a conversion tool, not an afterthought.** Made in Stone proves that a detailed, well-designed quote form captures better leads. The more information captured upfront, the faster Stonera can respond with an accurate quote, and speed-to-quote wins jobs.
