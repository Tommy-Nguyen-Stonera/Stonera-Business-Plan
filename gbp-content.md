### Post 2: How to Choose Between Engineered Stone and Natural Stone

**Title:** Engineered Stone vs Natural Stone. Which One's Right for You?

We get this question every week. Someone's building a new kitchen or renovating and they're stuck between engineered stone and natural stone. Here's the honest breakdown from someone who's been cutting both for over 25 years.

**Engineered stone** (brands like Caesarstone, Silestone, Smartstone, Essastone, Quantum Quartz) is made from crushed quartz mixed with resin. It's consistent in colour and pattern, which means what you see in the sample is what you get on your bench. It doesn't need sealing, it resists stains well, and it's generally more affordable. For most kitchens and bathrooms, it does the job perfectly.

**Natural stone** (marble, granite, quartzite) is quarried straight from the ground. Every slab is different. You get natural veining and depth that engineered stone can mimic but never fully match. Granite is tough as nails. Marble is softer and needs more care. Quartzite sits somewhere in between.

**Porcelain slabs** (Dekton, Neolith, Laminam) are another option worth knowing about. Ultra-thin, UV stable, and heat resistant. Great for outdoor areas and splashbacks.

There's no wrong answer. It depends on your budget, where it's going, and what look you're after. We're accredited with every major brand in Australia so we can work with whatever you choose.
