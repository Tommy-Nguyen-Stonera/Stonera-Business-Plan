### Key Findings

**What competitors are doing:**
- Almost every competitor uses white/grey/charcoal as their base. No surprises there.
- Gold accents are the most popular "premium" signal (Royal Stone, InForm, Made in Stone all use gold).
- Blue is the second most common accent (Harmony, Stone Benchtops Direct, Universal Stone).
- Nobody in this space is using green.

**How Stonera's veridian green (#2C4A3E) stacks up:**
- It is genuinely different. Not a single competitor in Sydney uses green as their primary accent. This is a real advantage.
- Green communicates reliability, earth, natural materials. It fits stone fabrication without trying to be luxury.
- The dark, muted tone avoids looking "eco/sustainability" (which a bright green would suggest). Instead it reads as grounded and serious.
- It separates Stonera from the gold = luxury crowd, which is exactly right for the "affordable quality" positioning.

**Verdict: Keep the green. It works.**

### Contrast Ratio Testing (WCAG Accessibility)

| Combination | Ratio | WCAG AA (4.5:1) | WCAG AAA (7:1) |
|------------|-------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Veridian #2C4A3E on White #FFFFFF | 9.74:1 | PASS | PASS |
| Veridian #2C4A3E on Stone #F7F5F2 | 8.95:1 | PASS | PASS |
| Charcoal #1A1A1A on Stone #F7F5F2 | 15.99:1 | PASS | PASS |
| Charcoal #1A1A1A on White #FFFFFF | 17.40:1 | PASS | PASS |
| Grey #6B6560 on Stone #F7F5F2 | 5.28:1 | PASS | NO |

All primary text combinations pass AAA. The grey (#6B6560) on stone background passes AA but not AAA. That grey should only be used for secondary/helper text, not body copy. This is fine for its intended use (captions, meta info).

### Palette Refinements (Minor Tweaks)

The current palette is solid. Only small suggestions:

1. **Add a darker green variant** (#1E3329) for hover states on buttons and links. Gives you a clear interactive state without introducing a new color.

2. **Consider a subtle warm accent** (#C4A882, a muted sand/stone tone) for occasional highlights, pull quotes, or section dividers. This stays within the natural stone family and gives the palette one more tool to work with. Use sparingly.

3. **Keep the grey #6B6560 for secondary text only.** It passes AA but not AAA, so don't use it for anything a user needs to read easily.

## 2. Typography Audit

### Current Pairing: DM Serif Display + Inter

**DM Serif Display** is a high-contrast transitional serif. Sharp, refined, reads well at large sizes. It was designed for display/headline use, not body text.

**Inter** is a workhorse sans-serif built for screens. Excellent readability at all sizes. Very widely used across tech, SaaS, and professional services.

### How This Pairing Works for a Trade Business

**Strengths:**
- The contrast between serif headings and sans-serif body is a proven, readable combination.
- DM Serif Display gives Stonera a more refined presence than most trade businesses, which tend to use generic sans-serif everything.
- Inter is incredibly readable and works perfectly for quotes, specs, contact info, and mobile.

**Potential concerns:**
- DM Serif Display leans toward editorial/design portfolios. It is a beautiful font, but it might feel slightly polished for a business that wants to come across as practical and down-to-earth.
- Inter is used on thousands of websites. It does not hurt the brand, but it does not help differentiate either.

### Competitor Typography

| Company | Heading Font | Body Font |
|---------|-------------|-----------|
| Candor Stone | Raleway | Raleway |
| Royal Stone | Playfair Display | Montserrat |
| InForm Stone | Poppins | Roboto |
| Made in Stone | Essonnes Text (serif) | Sans-serif (unspecified) |
| Stone Factory | Arial/Helvetica | Arial/Helvetica |
| Harmony Stone | System sans-serif | System sans-serif |
| Stone Benchtops Direct | PT Sans | Open Sans |
| Universal Stone | Open Sans | Open Sans |

**Observations:**
- Most competitors use generic sans-serif fonts (Poppins, Montserrat, Open Sans, Arial). Nothing memorable.
- Royal Stone uses Playfair Display (serif) which is similar territory to DM Serif Display but more ornate and luxury-coded.
- Made in Stone is the only other competitor with a distinctive serif heading font.
- Nobody is using DM Serif Display. Stonera's typography is already more distinctive than the competition.

### Verdict: Keep DM Serif Display + Inter. It works.

The pairing hits a good balance point. It is more polished than the typical trade business website (which helps Stonera stand out to builders and cabinet makers who are used to seeing rough-looking competitor sites), without tipping into luxury territory.

### Alternative Pairings (If You Want to Explore)

**Option A: Darker, more grounded feel**
- Headings: **Libre Baskerville** (a sturdy, traditional serif with more weight than DM Serif Display)
- Body: **Source Sans 3** (clean, professional, slightly warmer than Inter)
- Why: Feels more established and trade-appropriate. Less editorial, more "been doing this for 25 years."

**Option B: Modern and bold**
- Headings: **Sora** (geometric sans-serif with personality, strong at large sizes)
- Body: **Inter** (keep it)
- Why: Drops the serif entirely for a more contemporary, bold look. Good if you want to appeal to younger builders/renovators. Less traditional, more forward.

Recommendation: Stick with DM Serif Display + Inter unless you feel the brand needs to shift tone. The current pairing is distinctive in this market.

## 3. Tagline Evaluation

### Current Tagline: "Quality Stone Work. No Surprises."

### Competitor Taglines

| Company | Tagline / Key Message |
|---------|----------------------|
| Candor Stone | "Stone fabricators & installers in Sydney" |
| Royal Stone | "Most trusted stone benchtop manufacturer & fabricator in Sydney" |
| InForm Stone | "Solid experience, beautiful finishes" |
| Made in Stone | "We know stone. It's what we do." |
| Stone Benchtops Direct | "Your space deserves craftsmanship, not guesswork" |
| Sydney Stone Benchtops | "Generations of Craftsmanship" |
| Harmony Stone | "Sydney's Premier Stone Destination" |
| Universal Stone | "Sydney's trusted name for quality stone surfaces" |

### Analysis

**What competitors say:**
- "Trusted" and "quality" are used by everyone. Those words have basically zero impact in this market.
- Most taglines are either generic descriptions ("fabricators & installers") or vague prestige claims ("premier destination").
- Only two stand out: Made in Stone's "We know stone. It's what we do." (simple, confident) and Stone Benchtops Direct's "Your space deserves craftsmanship, not guesswork" (addresses a pain point).

**How "Quality Stone Work. No Surprises." compares:**
- The "No Surprises" part is the real strength here. It speaks directly to what builders and homeowners actually worry about: unexpected costs, delays, wrong measurements, poor communication. That is a genuine pain point this tagline addresses.
- "Quality Stone Work" is the weaker half. Every stone company claims quality. It does not add much.
- The two-sentence structure with periods gives it a punchy, no-nonsense rhythm. That fits the brand personality.
- It reads like something a tradesperson would say, not a marketing agency. That is a good thing for this market.

### Verdict: Decent tagline. The "No Surprises" half carries it.

### Alternative Options (Same Vibe)

If you want to explore stronger versions that keep the same straightforward, practical tone:

**Option 1: "Stone done right. Price upfront."**
- Hits both quality and transparency in fewer words.
- "Price upfront" is more specific than "no surprises" and directly addresses the biggest concern builders have.
- Reads like a promise, not a slogan.

**Option 2: "Measure twice. Cut once. Every time."**
- Uses a phrase every tradesperson knows and respects.
- Communicates precision and consistency without using the word "quality."
- Memorable and easy to say.
- Might work better as a secondary line or campaign phrase rather than the primary tagline.

**Option 3: "Good stone work, fair price, on time."**
- Three simple promises. No fluff.
- Covers the three things that actually matter to a builder booking a fabrication job.
- Very direct, very trade-appropriate.

Recommendation: The current tagline is serviceable. If you want to swap, Option 1 is the strongest replacement because it keeps the same structure and energy while being more specific. But this is not urgent. There is nothing wrong with what you have.

## 4. Logo Concepts

### Current Logo: "STONERA" in DM Serif Display, text only

### Concept A: Refined Text-Only with Typographic Detail

**Description:** Keep "STONERA" in DM Serif Display but add a single subtle detail to the letterforms. The "O" gets a thin horizontal line through its center (similar to the Greek theta or a stone slab cross-section), turning it into a mark that references layered stone without being obvious about it.

The rest of the letters stay untouched. The kerning is tightened slightly for a more cohesive block of text. Below the wordmark, a thin 1px line in veridian green (#2C4A3E) sits underneath, the same width as the text.

**What it communicates:** Precision, attention to detail, craftsmanship. The modified "O" is a quiet signal that this is a stone business without needing to spell it out with imagery.

**At small sizes (favicon, Instagram):** Use just the modified "O" with the line through it as the favicon. It is simple enough to read at 16x16px and still carries the stone reference. For Instagram profile pictures, use "S" from the wordmark set inside a square with a veridian green background.

---

### Concept B: Text + Simple Icon Mark

**Description:** "STONERA" wordmark sits to the right of a simple geometric icon. The icon is an abstracted stone slab stack: three thin horizontal rectangles stacked with small gaps between them, slightly offset from each other (like stone slabs leaning on a rack, viewed from the side). The rectangles use the veridian green (#2C4A3E) and the charcoal (#1A1A1A).

The icon sits in a clean circle or rounded square container. The overall layout is horizontal for headers/business cards and can stack vertically (icon on top, text below) for square formats.

**What it communicates:** Stone fabrication at its core. The stacked slabs reference the raw material and the workshop, grounding the brand in what Stonera actually does. The geometric style keeps it modern and clean without being fussy.

**At small sizes:** The stacked slab icon works well standalone as a favicon because it is just three rectangles. Simple geometry holds up at any size. For social media avatars, use the icon centered on the stone (#F7F5F2) background inside a circle.

---

### Concept C: Minimal Icon-Only Mark (For Social / Avatars)

**Description:** A single letterform "S" custom-drawn with characteristics of cut stone. The top and bottom terminals of the S are cut at clean 45-degree angles (like a mitre cut on a benchtop edge), while the curves remain smooth. The letter is rendered in veridian green (#2C4A3E) on a light stone (#F7F5F2) background.

Alternatively: a simple monogram combining "S" and a clean right-angle shape (like an L-bracket or benchtop corner profile) that nests into the bottom curve of the S. This creates a compact mark that says "stone" and "fabrication" in one symbol.

**What it communicates:** Modern, confident, identifiable. The cut angles on the S reference precision cutting, which is what Stonera does every day. It works as a brand stamp without needing the full name.

**At small sizes:** This is designed specifically for small formats. The clean curves and sharp terminal cuts remain readable even at 32x32px. It works as an app icon, favicon, watermark on project photos, or embroidered on work shirts.

---

### Logo Recommendation

Start with Concept A (refined text with the modified "O") as the primary logo. It is the lowest-risk change from what you have now and adds just enough distinction. Use Concept C (the custom S mark) as the social media avatar and favicon. You do not need to commit to Concept B unless you want a more "complete" logo system down the road.

## 5. Brand Guidelines Summary

### STONERA BRAND GUIDELINES

---

#### Logo

- **Primary logo:** "STONERA" wordmark in DM Serif Display, all caps
- **Minimum clear space:** Height of the letter "S" on all sides
- **Minimum size:** 120px wide for digital, 30mm wide for print
- **Approved backgrounds:** Stone (#F7F5F2), White (#FFFFFF), Charcoal (#1A1A1A)
- **On dark backgrounds:** Use white (#FFFFFF) logo text
- **On light backgrounds:** Use charcoal (#1A1A1A) logo text
- **Do not:** Stretch, rotate, add drop shadows, change the font, put the logo on busy photo backgrounds without a solid backing, change the letter spacing

---

#### Colors

| Role | Name | Hex | RGB | Usage |
|------|------|-----|-----|-------|
| Primary Accent | Veridian Green | #2C4A3E | 44, 74, 62 | Buttons, links, borders, highlights |
| Accent Hover | Deep Forest | #1E3329 | 30, 51, 41 | Button hover states, active states |
| Background | Stone | #F7F5F2 | 247, 245, 242 | Page backgrounds, cards |
| Primary Text | Charcoal | #1A1A1A | 26, 26, 26 | All body text, headings |
| Secondary Text | Warm Grey | #6B6560 | 107, 101, 96 | Captions, dates, helper text only |
| Border | Stone Border | #E0DCD7 | 224, 220, 215 | Dividers, card borders, input fields |

**Rules:**
- Never use the grey (#6B6560) for body text or anything users need to read at length
- Veridian green is for accents and interactive elements, not for large text blocks
- Keep backgrounds light (stone or white). Dark mode uses charcoal (#1A1A1A) as background with stone (#F7F5F2) text

---

#### Typography

| Use | Font | Weight | Size Range |
|-----|------|--------|------------|
| Headings (H1-H3) | DM Serif Display | Regular (400) | 28-48px |
| Subheadings (H4-H6) | Inter | Semi-bold (600) | 18-24px |
| Body text | Inter | Regular (400) | 16px |
| Small text / captions | Inter | Regular (400) | 14px |
| Buttons | Inter | Medium (500) | 14-16px, uppercase, letter-spacing 0.5px |

**Rules:**
- DM Serif Display is for headings only. Never use it for body text or buttons.
- Body text line-height: 1.6
- Heading line-height: 1.2
- Max reading width for body text: 680px

---

#### Tone of Voice

Stonera talks like a good tradesperson talks to a builder they respect: straight, clear, and honest.

**Do:**
- Be direct. Say what you mean in as few words as possible.
- Use plain language. "We cut stone" not "We craft bespoke stone solutions."
- Lead with facts: years of experience, brands worked with, turnaround times.
- Sound confident without being arrogant. Let the work speak.
- Write like you would talk to a builder over the phone.

**Do not:**
- Use marketing speak ("unparalleled," "bespoke," "curated," "elevate")
- Make vague promises ("world-class service," "exceeding expectations")
- Over-explain. If a builder knows what a mitre join is, you do not need to define it.
- Use exclamation marks or hype language
- Sound like a luxury brand. Stonera is affordable quality, not a marble palace.

**Examples:**

| Instead of this | Write this |
|----------------|-----------|
| "We provide bespoke stone fabrication solutions tailored to your unique needs" | "We cut and install stone benchtops. All brands, all stone types." |
| "Our team of experienced craftsmen deliver unparalleled quality" | "25 years in the trade. We know what we are doing." |
| "Contact us today for a free consultation!" | "Call for a quote. Same-day response." |

---

#### Brand Do's and Don'ts

**Do:**
- Use the veridian green consistently as the main accent color everywhere
- Keep layouts clean with plenty of white space
- Show real project photos, not stock images
- Use the stone (#F7F5F2) background as the default, not pure white
- Keep the brand feeling grounded and practical

**Do not:**
- Add gold accents (that is what every other stone company does)
- Use gradients, gloss effects, or heavy drop shadows
- Put text over busy stone texture photos without a solid overlay
- Mix in extra colors outside the palette
- Use script fonts, decorative fonts, or anything that looks like a wedding invitation
- Make things look more expensive than they are. The brand is honest pricing, not hidden luxury.
